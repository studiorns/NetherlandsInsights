Netherlands Market Metrics & Insights

Department of Culture and Tourism - Abu Dhabi

Market Analysis and Performance Indicators

Introduction

This guide provides detailed explanations of the key metrics and insights used in our market analysis dashboards. It is designed to help decision-makers understand the technical aspects of our market performance indicators and their business implications.

Each metric is explained in terms of:

  • What it measures
  • How it's calculated
  • How to interpret different values
  • Business implications and actionable insights
  • Practical examples where applicable

The metrics are organized into categories that align with our dashboard structure, making it easy to reference when reviewing market performance.

Brand Health Metrics

Brand health metrics measure consumer perceptions and attitudes toward our destination. These metrics are leading indicators that typically precede changes in actual travel behavior.

Awareness

61.22% (+3.22pp)

Awareness measures the percentage of the target audience who are aware of Abu Dhabi as a Cultural and Sea & Sun travel destination.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which international holiday destinations offering a combination of both cultural and sun & beach activities are you aware of?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of the total surveyed population.

Interpretation:
  • 90%+: Excellent awareness, approaching market saturation
  • 70-90%: Strong awareness, but room for improvement
  • 50-70%: Moderate awareness, significant opportunity for growth
  • <50%: Low awareness, major opportunity but requires substantial investment
Business Implications:
  • At 61.22%, Netherlands has moderate awareness of Abu Dhabi, indicating good market penetration but significant room for growth.
  • The +3.22pp year-over-year growth suggests continued improvement in awareness levels.
  • This moderate awareness level suggests that continued investment in awareness-building campaigns is necessary to move into the "strong awareness" category (70-90%).
  • The positive trend indicates that current marketing efforts are effective at building awareness, but there is still a substantial portion of the Dutch market that is not aware of Abu Dhabi as a destination.
  • Marketing should maintain awareness-building efforts while increasingly focusing on conversion to consideration and intent.
  • The positive trend in awareness provides a solid foundation for more targeted marketing efforts aimed at improving consideration and intent metrics.

Consideration

29.87% (+3.87pp)

Consideration measures the percentage of aware consumers who would consider travelling to Abu Dhabi in the next 3 years.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations would you like to visit in the next 3 years?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are aware of Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 70%+: Excellent consideration, strong position in consumer mindset
  • 50-70%: Good consideration, competitive position
  • 30-50%: Moderate consideration, significant barriers exist
  • <30%: Low consideration, major perception issues to address
Business Implications:
  • At 29.87%, consideration is just below the moderate range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from awareness to consideration.
  • The +3.87pp increase shows a strong positive trend that should be leveraged.
  • This suggests a significant gap between awareness (61.22%) and consideration (29.87%), indicating that while many Dutch travelers know about Abu Dhabi, fewer are actively considering it as a destination.
  • The strong positive growth in consideration (+3.87pp) suggests that recent marketing efforts are effectively addressing some barriers to consideration.
  • Marketing should focus on addressing the specific barriers that prevent aware consumers from considering Abu Dhabi, particularly the top barriers identified in market research: destination preference (28%), expense concerns (25%), safety concerns (21%), and authenticity concerns (21%).
  • The positive trend in consideration, coupled with the positive trend in awareness, suggests that the Netherlands market is responding well to current marketing efforts and has significant growth potential.

Intent

14.81% (+0.81pp)

Intent measures the percentage of considering consumers who intend to visit Abu Dhabi in the next 12 months.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "Which of the below holiday destinations are you likely to visit in the next 12 months?" The percentage represents those who respond positively out of those who are considering Abu Dhabi.

Interpretation:
  • 50%+: Excellent intent, very strong conversion potential
  • 30-50%: Good intent, healthy conversion potential
  • 20-30%: Moderate intent, conversion challenges exist
  • <20%: Low intent, significant conversion barriers
Business Implications:
  • At 14.81%, intent is in the low range, indicating significant barriers to conversion from consideration to intent.
  • The +0.81pp increase shows a positive but modest trend that requires attention.
  • This suggests that even among those who consider Abu Dhabi, relatively few have immediate plans to visit.
  • The modest growth in intent (+0.81pp) compared to the stronger growth in consideration (+3.87pp) indicates that while more Dutch travelers are considering Abu Dhabi, there are still significant barriers to converting that consideration into actual travel intent.
  • Marketing should focus on creating urgency and addressing practical barriers to booking (visa process, flight availability, etc.).
  • The low intent level suggests that tactical, conversion-focused campaigns are needed to convert the growing consideration into actual travel intent.

Intent/Consideration Ratio

0.50 (-0.04)

This ratio measures the efficiency of converting consideration to intent, indicating how effectively we move consumers down the funnel.

How It's Calculated:

Intent percentage divided by Consideration percentage. For example: 14.81% ÷ 29.87% = 0.50

Interpretation:
  • 0.8+: Excellent conversion efficiency, minimal drop-off
  • 0.6-0.8: Good conversion efficiency
  • 0.4-0.6: Moderate conversion efficiency, significant drop-off
  • <0.4: Poor conversion efficiency, major funnel leakage
Business Implications:
  • At 0.50, the conversion from consideration to intent is moderate, indicating that 50% of those who consider Abu Dhabi intend to visit in the next 12 months.
  • The decrease of -0.04 suggests a slight deterioration in conversion efficiency.
  • This indicates that while both consideration and intent are growing, consideration is growing faster than intent, resulting in a slightly reduced ratio.
  • The moderate ratio suggests that once Dutch travelers consider Abu Dhabi, they have a reasonable likelihood of converting to actual intent to visit, but there is significant room for improvement.
  • Marketing should focus on understanding and addressing the specific barriers that are preventing consideration from converting to intent.
  • The slight decrease in this ratio (-0.04) is a concerning sign that should be monitored closely, as it suggests that the funnel is becoming less efficient at converting consideration to intent.

Market Characteristics

Market characteristics describe fundamental travel behaviors and patterns specific to the Netherlands market. These metrics help inform tactical marketing decisions and campaign timing.

Average Length of Stay

3.5 days

The average number of nights Dutch travelers spend in Abu Dhabi per visit.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel guest data and visitor surveys (DVS), calculated as the sum of all nights stayed divided by the number of visitors.

Business Implications:
  • The 3.5-day average stay indicates a short-to-medium length vacation timeframe, typical of many European markets.
  • This suggests marketing should focus on compact itineraries and experiences that can be enjoyed within a 3-4 day timeframe.
  • Package offerings should be designed around this timeframe to maximize appeal.
  • There's an opportunity to develop weekend-plus packages that include Abu Dhabi as a short break destination.
  • Hotel partners should be encouraged to develop 3-4 day packages with all-inclusive options to appeal to this market.
  • The relatively short stay suggests a need for high-impact experiences that deliver maximum value in a limited time.

Booking Window

70 days

The average time between booking and arrival for Dutch travelers visiting Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on booking data from hotels, flight booking engines and OTAs, calculated as the average number of days between booking confirmation and arrival date.

Business Implications:
  • The 70-day booking window is relatively long, indicating a market that plans trips well in advance.
  • Marketing campaigns should be timed to run approximately 2-3 months before target travel periods.
  • This window allows for considered decision-making and comparison shopping by travelers.
  • Early booking incentives are likely to be effective for this market.
  • The long booking window suggests that both digital and traditional media can be effective, as there is sufficient time for consumers to respond to marketing messages.
  • This extended planning period also provides opportunities for upselling and package enhancements over time.
Strategic Example:

For the December-February peak season, major campaign pushes should begin in September-October to align with the 70-day booking window. Early booking discounts offered 85+ days in advance could be particularly effective for this market, encouraging even earlier commitments.

Peak Months (Hotel Guests)

Feb & Dec

The months with the highest volume of Dutch hotel guests in Abu Dhabi.

How It's Calculated:

Based on hotel occupancy data, identifying the months with the highest number of Dutch guests.

Business Implications:
  • February (3,070 guests) and December (3,280 guests) represent the clear peak season for Dutch travelers.
  • This winter peak aligns with the Netherlands' cold weather season, suggesting a strong "sun-seeking" motivation.
  • Marketing should ensure sufficient inventory and capacity during these months.
  • Pricing strategies can be optimized for these high-demand periods.
  • There's an opportunity to develop shoulder season campaigns (January, March, November) to extend the peak periods.
  • Summer months represent a significant growth opportunity through targeted campaigns highlighting indoor activities and special summer offers, especially given the elimination of weather concerns as a barrier (-21pp).

Advanced Statistical Metrics

These advanced metrics provide deeper insights into market dynamics, helping to quantify relationships between marketing activities and outcomes, forecast accuracy, and market behavior patterns.

Seasonality Index

1.9 (high seasonal variation)

A statistical measure that quantifies the degree of seasonal variation in the Netherlands travel market data.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated by dividing the highest monthly value by the average monthly value in a year. For example, if December has a travel query index of 18.65 (the highest month) and the average monthly index is around 9.82, the seasonality index would be approximately 1.9 (18.65 ÷ 9.82).

Interpretation:
  • 1.0-1.3: Low seasonal variation (relatively flat demand throughout the year)
  • 1.3-1.6: Moderate seasonal variation (noticeable peaks and troughs)
  • 1.6-2.0: High seasonal variation (significant peaks and troughs)
  • 2.0+: Extreme seasonal variation (very pronounced peaks and low seasons)
Business Implications:
  • The 1.9 value indicates that the market has high seasonal fluctuations, with peak months having about 90% higher travel query activity than the average month.
  • This is significant enough to warrant strong seasonal marketing strategies.
  • Marketing efforts should be concentrated before peak periods (considering the 70-day booking window).
  • There's also opportunity to grow travel interest during off-peak months through targeted campaigns.
  • Resource allocation should follow this seasonal pattern, with increased investment before peak periods.
  • The high seasonality suggests a need for differential pricing and promotion strategies across the year.

Media Elasticity

0.37 (moderate impact)

A marketing metric that measures how responsive travel queries are to changes in media impressions for the Netherlands market.

How It's Calculated:

Calculated using regression analysis on historical data, comparing the percentage change in travel queries to the percentage change in media impressions over time.

Formula: Media Elasticity = % Change in Travel Queries ÷ % Change in Media Impressions

For the Netherlands market: 35.2% ÷ 95.9% = 0.37

Interpretation:
  • <0.3: Low impact (minimal response to media investment)
  • 0.3-0.6: Moderate impact (reasonable response to media investment)
  • 0.6-1.0: High impact (strong response to media investment)
  • >1.0: Very high impact (exceptional response to media investment)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.37 elasticity means that for every 10% increase in media impressions, travel queries increase by approximately 3.7%.
  • This indicates that media investments drive travel queries with moderate efficiency.
  • The relationship is positive but not particularly strong (just above 0.3).
  • Media campaigns will generate positive ROI but with moderate efficiency.
  • Doubling media spend will not double travel queries, but will produce modest growth.
  • The moderate elasticity suggests that the Netherlands market is somewhat responsive to media campaigns, but other factors also significantly influence travel decisions.
  • This moderate responsiveness suggests that media investments should be complemented by other marketing approaches, such as addressing specific barriers to visitation.
Strategic Example:

If you're planning to increase media impressions by 20% for a campaign, you can expect approximately a 7.4% increase in travel queries (20% × 0.37 = 7.4%). This helps set realistic expectations for campaign performance and ROI calculations.

YoY Growth Consistency

0.78 (high consistency)

A statistical measure that evaluates how uniform or predictable the year-over-year growth patterns are for the Netherlands travel market.

How It's Calculated:

Typically calculated using a statistical measure like the coefficient of variation (inverse) or correlation coefficient of YoY growth rates across different time periods. The scale ranges from 0 to 1, where 0 represents completely inconsistent growth (highly volatile) and 1 represents perfectly consistent growth (same growth rate across all periods).

Interpretation:
  • <0.5: Low consistency (high volatility, unpredictable patterns)
  • 0.5-0.7: Moderate consistency (some predictability with variations)
  • 0.7-0.9: High consistency (reliable patterns with minor variations)
  • >0.9: Very high consistency (highly predictable patterns)
Business Implications:
  • The 0.78 consistency indicates that there is high predictability in how the market behaves year-over-year.
  • Forecasting can be done with high confidence.
  • Most months will show similar growth patterns to previous years.
  • Only occasional months may show unexpected variations requiring adaptive strategies.
  • Marketing teams can plan with greater certainty about expected outcomes.
  • Historical patterns provide reliable guidance for future planning.
  • This high consistency (0.78) contributes to a narrow forecast confidence interval of ±7.2%.

Forecast Confidence Interval

±7.2%

A statistical measure indicating the range within which we expect the actual 2025 travel query values to fall with a certain level of confidence (typically 95%).

How It's Calculated:

Derived from statistical analysis of historical forecast accuracy, market volatility, and model parameters. It represents the standard error of the forecast multiplied by a factor corresponding to the desired confidence level (typically 1.96 for 95% confidence).

Interpretation:
  • <5%: Very high forecast precision, highly reliable predictions
  • 5-10%: Good forecast precision, reliable predictions with moderate variance
  • 10-15%: Moderate forecast precision, usable predictions with significant variance
  • >15%: Low forecast precision, high uncertainty in predictions
Business Implications:
  • The ±7.2% confidence interval means that if we forecast a travel query index of 150.0 for a particular month, the actual value is expected to fall between 139.2 and 160.8 with 95% confidence.
  • This relatively narrow interval indicates a reliable forecast for the Netherlands market.
  • Marketing teams should plan with appropriate contingencies, knowing the potential range of outcomes.
  • Budget allocations should include flexibility to adjust based on actual performance within this range.
  • The confidence interval suggests that forecasts can be used with good confidence for planning purposes.

Barriers to Visitation

Understanding why potential visitors choose not to travel to Abu Dhabi is crucial for developing targeted marketing strategies that address specific concerns and misconceptions.

Top Barriers to Visit

5 key factors

The primary reasons Dutch travelers give for not considering Abu Dhabi as a travel destination, based on survey research.

How It's Calculated:

Based on survey responses to questions like "What are the main reasons you would not consider traveling to Abu Dhabi?" The percentages represent the proportion of respondents who selected each reason.

Current Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Destination Preference: 28% (+4pp) prefer other destinations
  • Expense Concerns: 25% (-5pp) think it's too expensive
  • Safety Concerns: 21% (+8pp) perceive it as unsafe
  • Authenticity Concerns: 21% (0pp) find it too fake/not authentic
  • Lack of Knowledge: 16% (+9pp) don't know enough about the place
Business Implications:
  • Destination Preference (28%, +4pp): This increase indicates growing competition from alternative destinations. Marketing should emphasize Abu Dhabi's unique selling points and differentiation from competing destinations.
  • Expense Concerns (25%, -5pp): The decrease suggests that value perception is improving, but cost remains a significant barrier. Continue to emphasize value for money and consider targeted promotions.
  • Safety Concerns (21%, +8pp): This substantial increase indicates a deteriorating perception of safety that must be addressed. Marketing should emphasize Abu Dhabi's strong safety record and low crime rates.
  • Authenticity Concerns (21%, 0pp): This stable barrier suggests a persistent perception issue that needs to be addressed through showcasing authentic cultural experiences and local interactions.
  • Lack of Knowledge (16%, +9pp): This significant increase indicates a growing information gap. Marketing should focus on educational content about Abu Dhabi's attractions, culture, and experiences.
Strategic Recommendation:

Based on these barriers, a "Discover Authentic Abu Dhabi" campaign highlighting genuine cultural experiences (addressing the "Authenticity Concerns" barrier) combined with educational content about the destination (addressing the "Lack of Knowledge" barrier) and safety information (addressing the "Safety Concerns" barrier) would address three of the five top barriers to visitation from the Netherlands market.

Increasing Barriers

5 worsening factors

Barriers to visitation that have shown significant deterioration over time, representing challenges that need to be addressed.

Worsening Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Only for Younger People: 15% (+15pp) think it's only for younger people
  • No Direct Flights: 14% (+13pp) believe there are no direct flights
  • Lack of Knowledge: 16% (+9pp) don't know enough about the place
  • Safety Concerns: 21% (+8pp) perceive it as unsafe
  • Destination Preference: 28% (+4pp) prefer other destinations
Business Implications:
  • The substantial increase in "Only for Younger People" (+15pp) represents a critical new challenge, indicating a significant shift in perception that requires immediate attention.
  • The significant increase in "No Direct Flights" (+13pp) suggests a growing misconception about flight connectivity that must be addressed through targeted communications.
  • The increase in "Lack of Knowledge" (+9pp) indicates a growing information gap that requires educational content and awareness-building campaigns.
  • The substantial increase in "Safety Concerns" (+8pp) indicates a serious deterioration in safety perception that must be addressed through targeted communications.
  • The increase in "Destination Preference" (+4pp) suggests growing competition from alternative destinations that must be addressed through differentiation strategies.
  • These worsening barriers should be prioritized in marketing communications, with particular focus on addressing age perception, flight connectivity, and destination awareness.

Improving Barriers

Key positive changes

Barriers to visitation that have shown improvement over time, representing areas of progress.

Improving Barriers (Q4 2024 vs Q4 2023):
  • Weather Concerns: 0% (-21pp) think the weather is not good
  • Value for Money: 10% (-18pp) think it's not value for money
  • Restrictive Laws/Customs: 16% (-14pp) find laws/customs too restrictive
  • Bad Experiences: 8% (-11pp) heard others had bad experiences
Business Implications:
  • The dramatic decrease in "Weather Concerns" (-21pp) represents a significant positive shift in perception, likely reflecting the impact of campaigns highlighting indoor attractions and climate-controlled environments.
  • The substantial decrease in "Value for Money" (-18pp) suggests that value perception has improved significantly, likely reflecting the impact of value-focused marketing and competitive pricing strategies.
  • The substantial decrease in "Restrictive Laws/Customs" (-14pp) represents a significant positive shift in perception, likely reflecting the impact of recent liberalization of laws and customs in Abu Dhabi.
  • The significant decrease in "Bad Experiences" (-11pp) suggests that word-of-mouth is improving, likely reflecting improved visitor experiences and service quality.
  • Marketing should leverage these improving barriers, particularly the elimination of weather concerns, which could be especially effective in promoting summer travel to address the seasonal weakness in June-August.
  • These improving barriers demonstrate that perceptions can be changed through targeted communications and policy changes, providing a model for addressing the currently worsening barriers.

Conclusion

This guide provides a comprehensive explanation of the key metrics used in our market analysis for the Netherlands. By understanding these metrics and their business implications, DCT - Abu Dhabi can make more informed strategic marketing decisions.

The Netherlands market shows several distinctive characteristics:

  • Moderate awareness (61.22%) with growing consideration (29.87%, +3.87pp) and intent (14.81%, +0.81pp), indicating positive momentum but significant barriers to conversion
  • A moderate intent-to-consideration ratio (0.50) indicating reasonable funnel conversion once consideration is achieved, but with a concerning slight decrease (-0.04)
  • A relatively short average stay (3.5 days) indicating short-break vacation patterns
  • A long booking window (70 days) allowing for effective campaign planning
  • Strong winter seasonality with peaks in February and December
  • Moderate media elasticity (0.37) suggesting reasonable but not strong response to media investments
  • Significant barriers including destination preference (28%), expense concerns (25%), and safety concerns (21%)
  • Concerning growth in "only for younger people" (+15pp), "no direct flights" (+13pp), and "lack of knowledge" (+9pp)
  • Positive elimination of weather concerns (-21pp) and significant improvements in value perception (-18pp)

These insights suggest a strategic focus on:

  • Addressing the significant gap between awareness and consideration through targeted messaging that addresses specific barriers
  • Developing a multi-generational appeal campaign to address the significant increase in "only for younger people" perception
  • Highlighting flight connectivity to address the growing "no direct flights" perception
  • Creating educational content to address the growing "lack of knowledge" barrier
  • Emphasizing Abu Dhabi's safety record and security measures to address growing safety concerns
  • Developing compact itineraries that maximize the typical 3.5-day stay, focusing on high-impact experiences
  • Timing campaigns to align with the 70-day booking window, particularly for the December-February peak period
  • Leveraging the elimination of weather concerns to promote summer travel and address the seasonal weakness in June-August
  • Implementing a dynamic media allocation model that adjusts spend based on the 2-month lag effect between media impressions and travel queries

With these insights and ongoing monitoring of these metrics, DCT - Abu Dhabi can formulate more effective marketing strategies and increase our return on marketing investment in the Netherlands market. The significant increase in planned media impressions for 2025 (293.0M, +95.9% YoY) should drive substantial growth in travel queries, particularly for the December peak period which is projected to reach 18.65 in the moderate scenario (a 79% increase from December 2024).
